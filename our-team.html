Shairoz Mistry

Early Childhood Development Advisor
Shairoz Mistry is an education and community impact leader with expertise in early childhood development, parent education, educator coaching, quality improvement systems, and culturally responsive programming. She currently serves as Senior Director of Community Impact at Children NMuseum Houston, where she leads early childhood initiatives, community outreach, and evaluation efforts serving more approximately 1 million children and families annually.

Her career spans leadership roles in the United States and the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC), including Head of the Aga Khan Education Portfolio for the GCC, Curriculum Developer for the Aga Khan Education Portfolio (GCC), Manager of Mentors at Collaborative for Children, and National Project Manager for Pre-primary Teacher Mentoring and Coaching program at Ismaili Tariqa and Religious Education Board (ITREB) USA. At Collaborative for Children, she led a team of mentors supporting teaching and administrative quality improvement systems across 13 counties surrounding Greater Houston, primarily serving child care programs in low-income communities.

Shairoz is passionate about ensuring all children have access to high-quality early learning experiences and enter school ready to thrive, regardless of their socioeconomic, cultural, or linguistic background.

As a Worldwide Education Foundation Board Member, she is committed to advancing equitable educational opportunities and strengthening systems that support children, families, and educators worldwide.

Dr. Anita Hasni Mohammad

Herzing-Daya Research & Learning Hub Advisor
Dr. Anita Hasni Mohammad is a developmental psychologist, researcher, and international development consultant with deep expertise in early childhood development, child psychology, and education systems. She holds a PhD from Georgia State University, where her research examined the cognitive and social foundations of early childhood — including original work on Theory of Mind in preschoolers, investigating how young children develop social awareness and make sense of the world through visual and auditory experience. Her academic work spans developmental milestones, sensitive learning periods, and the ethical dimensions of research with children.

Bridging research and public policy, Dr. Mohammad worked as a Lead Research and Policy Analyst with the Georgia Department of Early Care and Learning (DECAL), where she led continuous quality improvement initiatives for state-level early education programs. She managed and evaluated Early Head Start–Child Care Partnerships, with a particular focus on sustaining high-quality services for vulnerable families during the disruptions of the COVID-19 pandemic.

In 2021, Dr. Mohammad founded Tesseract Global Consulting, a research and evaluation firm dedicated to translating complex data into accessible, actionable knowledge for practitioners, policymakers, and institutions. Through Tesseract Global, she has partnered with multiple United Nations evaluation offices — including contributing to system-wide gender integration evaluations and analyzing global progress toward Sustainable Development Goal 5: Gender Equality. She also serves on Georgia Tech’s College of Sciences Advisory Board and on community advisory committees focused on maternal and infant health, reflecting her commitment to science in service of human potential and well-being.

Dr. Mohammad brings to the Herzing-Daya Research and Learning Hub a rare combination of rigorous scholarship, on-the-ground policy experience, and global development perspective. Her expertise in early childhood frameworks and systemic education evaluation makes her an invaluable guide as the Hub works to generate knowledge from the mountains — and translate it into impact for the world.
